What Makes a Resume Template ATS-Friendly

An ATS-friendly resume template is one that allows applicant tracking software to correctly parse, categorize, and score every piece of information on your resume. That means the software can accurately extract your name, contact information, job titles, employment dates, education, skills, and achievements without scrambling, skipping, or misreading anything.

ATS parsers read resumes as a linear stream of text, from top to bottom. They rely on standard section headings to know what type of information they are processing. They cannot interpret graphics, read text inside images, reliably parse multi-column layouts, or extract content from tables and text boxes.

An ATS-compatible template therefore prioritizes machine readability without sacrificing human readability. The result is a clean, professional document that works for both audiences. Here are the core requirements:

  • Single-column layout with content flowing linearly from top to bottom
  • Standard section headings that every ATS recognizes
  • Plain text formatting with bold, bullets, and simple line breaks
  • No tables, text boxes, columns, graphics, icons, or images
  • Standard fonts in 10-12pt size
  • Contact information in the document body, not in headers or footers
  • Consistent date formatting throughout

Section-by-Section Breakdown

Contact Information

Place your full name on the first line of the document body in a larger or bold font. Below it, list your city and state (full street address is no longer necessary), phone number, email address, and LinkedIn URL. Do not place this information in the document header or footer. Taleo and several other ATS platforms cannot read header and footer content, which means your name and email could be invisible to the system.

Professional Summary

Write 3-4 lines that include your target job title, years of experience, top skills, and one quantified achievement. This section should be rewritten for each application to mirror the specific role. The summary is prime real estate for keywords because it is one of the first sections the ATS parses and recruiters read.

Work Experience

List positions in reverse chronological order. For each role, include the job title, company name, location, and employment dates on a single line. Follow with 3-5 bullet points that start with strong action verbs and include quantified results wherever possible. This is the section that carries the most weight with both ATS scoring and human reviewers.

Format your dates consistently. Use "Month Year - Month Year" or "MM/YYYY - MM/YYYY" throughout. Do not mix formats. The ATS uses dates to calculate your total years of experience and tenure at each company.

Education

Include the degree name, university, location, and graduation year. For recent graduates, you can add GPA (if above 3.5), relevant coursework, and academic honors. For experienced professionals, education typically goes after work experience and can be kept brief.

Skills

Organize skills by category (Technical, Tools, Methodologies, Certifications). List specific, concrete skills rather than vague descriptors. "Python, SQL, Tableau, Excel" is parseable. "Excellent analytical skills" is not a keyword the ATS is scanning for. For a deep dive on this section, see our guide on how to write the skills section for ATS.

Certifications (Optional)

If you hold professional certifications relevant to your field, list them with the certification name, issuing organization, and year obtained. Certifications are high-value keywords that ATS systems match directly against job requirements.

The 10 Formatting Rules Every ATS Resume Must Follow

These rules apply regardless of which ATS resume template you use. Violate any of them and you risk having your resume misread or rejected. For a comprehensive guide, see our ATS resume format guide.

  1. Use a single-column layout. No sidebars, no two-column designs. The ATS reads left to right, top to bottom. Columns create chaos in the parser's output.
  2. Use standard section headings. Work Experience, Education, Skills, Professional Summary, Certifications. These are the headings every ATS recognizes. Creative alternatives like "My Journey" or "Toolkit" will be miscategorized or ignored.
  3. Use standard fonts. Arial, Calibri, Georgia, Verdana, or Times New Roman in 10-12pt. No decorative, condensed, or custom-installed fonts.
  4. Use simple bullet points. Round bullets or dashes. No custom symbols, checkmarks, or icon-based bullets.
  5. Avoid tables and text boxes. Even for seemingly simple layouts, tables can scramble content order in the parser's output.
  6. No graphics, icons, logos, or photos. These are invisible to ATS and can disrupt parsing of nearby text.
  7. Place contact info in the document body. Never in headers or footers.
  8. Use consistent date formatting. Pick one format and use it throughout: "January 2024 - Present" or "01/2024 - Present."
  9. Keep margins between 0.5 and 1 inch. Extremely narrow margins can cause text to be cut off when the ATS renders your resume.
  10. Save in the right format. Submit as .docx for maximum compatibility, or a text-based .pdf. Never .pages, .rtf, or image-based files.

What to Avoid in Your Template

Understanding what not to do is as important as knowing the right format. Here are the most common template mistakes that cause ATS rejection:

Do

  • Single-column, linear layout
  • Standard headings (Work Experience, Skills)
  • Plain text with bold for emphasis
  • Simple round bullet points
  • Contact info in document body
  • Standard fonts at 10-12pt
  • Consistent date format throughout
  • White or light background

Avoid

  • Two-column or sidebar layouts
  • Creative section headings
  • Tables, text boxes, or frames
  • Icons, logos, or skill-level bars
  • Contact info in headers/footers
  • Decorative or custom fonts
  • Mixed date formats
  • Colored backgrounds or sections

A note on design-focused resume templates: Templates from Canva, Creative Market, Etsy, and similar platforms are often visually striking but ATS-hostile. They typically use text boxes, columns, graphics, and non-standard formatting that ATS parsers cannot process. If you love a particular design template, use it for in-person networking or portfolio purposes, but always have a separate ATS-optimized version for online applications.

File Format: PDF vs. Word

This is one of the most debated topics in resume optimization. Here is the straightforward answer:

  • .docx (Word) is the safest choice for ATS compatibility. Every major ATS can parse Word documents reliably. If the job posting does not specify a format, default to .docx.
  • .pdf is fine for most modern ATS platforms (Greenhouse, Lever, Workday). However, older systems may struggle with certain PDF structures. If you submit a PDF, make sure the text is selectable (not a scanned image) and avoid PDFs generated from design tools that embed text as vector graphics.
  • Never submit .pages, .rtf, .doc (old Word format), .jpg, .png, or any other format. These are either unsupported or unreliably parsed.

A quick test for your PDF: open it and try to select and copy a paragraph of text. If you can copy the text cleanly, the ATS can likely read it. If you cannot, the ATS cannot either.

The best resume template is one that looks professional to a human and reads perfectly to a machine. Those two goals are not in conflict.

How to Test Your Template

After filling in the template with your content, do not skip this step. Testing your resume before submitting ensures you catch formatting issues and keyword gaps that would otherwise cost you interviews.

Pre-Submission Checklist

  • Contact information is in the document body, not in a header or footer
  • All section headings use standard names (Work Experience, Education, Skills)
  • Layout is single-column with no tables, text boxes, or sidebars
  • Font is standard (Arial, Calibri, etc.) at 10-12pt
  • Dates are formatted consistently throughout
  • No graphics, icons, logos, photos, or skill-level bars
  • File is saved as .docx or text-based .pdf
  • Text can be selected and copied cleanly from the document
  • Resume has been scanned through an ATS checker with the target job description
