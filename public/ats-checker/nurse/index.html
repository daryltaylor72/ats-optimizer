ATS Resume Checker for Nurses

Ensure your nursing resume passes hospital and healthcare ATS filters so your clinical experience actually reaches hiring managers.

Why ATS Screening Is Critical in Healthcare Hiring

Healthcare is one of the largest employers in the world, and major hospital systems like HCA, Kaiser Permanente, and the VA process thousands of nursing applications through ATS platforms every month. Whether you're an RN, LPN, or NP, your resume must clear automated filters before a nurse recruiter ever sees it.

Nursing resumes face a unique parsing challenge: they're heavy with clinical abbreviations, certifications, and unit-specific terminology that ATS systems may not recognize if formatted incorrectly. A resume that reads perfectly to a fellow nurse can score poorly in an ATS because licensure details are embedded in tables, or certifications are listed using only acronyms the system doesn't map to the job posting.

Essential Keywords for Nursing Resumes

Healthcare ATS systems scan for credentials, clinical skills, and compliance terminology. Make sure your resume includes these high-priority keywords matched to your specialty:

  • Registered Nurse (RN) / BSN
  • Patient Assessment
  • Electronic Health Records (EHR)
  • HIPAA Compliance
  • BLS / ACLS Certification
  • Medication Administration
  • Care Plan Development
  • Clinical Documentation

Always spell out your certifications fully, then include the abbreviation in parentheses: "Basic Life Support (BLS)" and "Advanced Cardiovascular Life Support (ACLS)." Many ATS systems search for either form, and covering both maximizes your match rate.

Common ATS Mistakes Nurses Make

Putting license numbers in headers or footers. ATS parsers typically skip headers and footers. Your RN license number, NPI, and state credentials should be in the body of the resume, ideally in a dedicated "Licenses & Certifications" section.

Using nursing-specific jargon without standard terms. If your unit calls it "med-surg," make sure you also write "Medical-Surgical Nursing" somewhere on the resume. ATS systems may not recognize informal shorthand.

Listing facilities without context. Don't assume the ATS knows that "Johns Hopkins Bayview" is a Level I trauma center. Include bed count, unit type, and patient population details — these are keywords that recruiters configure their ATS to search for.

Omitting quantifiable patient care metrics. Instead of "Provided patient care," write "Managed care for 6-8 patients per shift in a 32-bed ICU, maintaining 98% medication administration accuracy." Numbers help both ATS ranking and recruiter engagement.
